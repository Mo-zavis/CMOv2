# 5 Ways to Reduce No-Shows During Ramadan

*Published: March 8, 2026 | Reading time: 4 minutes*

No-show rates in healthcare clinics typically spike during Ramadan. Patients juggle altered sleep patterns, social obligations, and physical fatigue from fasting. The good news: with a few targeted adjustments, your practice can maintain, and even improve, attendance rates during the holy month.

## Why No-Shows Increase During Ramadan

The shift in daily routine during Ramadan is significant. Patients wake earlier for Suhoor, work through the day without food or water, and often stay up later for Taraweeh prayers and family gatherings. Daytime appointments, especially those in the late afternoon, become harder to keep.

Understanding this pattern is the first step to solving it.

## 1. Shift Appointment Windows to Match Fasting Schedules

Morning slots (8-11 AM) see the highest attendance during Ramadan, when patients have recently eaten at Suhoor and energy levels are reasonable. Post-Iftar evening slots (8-10 PM) are the second-best window.

The danger zone is 2-5 PM, when fatigue and dehydration peak. If you can reduce appointments in this window, you will see immediate improvement in attendance.

## 2. Send Reminders at the Right Time

Generic 9 AM reminders may land when patients are sleeping after a late night. Optimize your reminder schedule:
- **24-hour reminder**: Send at 9 PM (post-Iftar, when patients are alert)
- **Same-day reminder**: Send at 7 AM (after Suhoor, before the day begins)
- **SMS over email**: Open rates for SMS remain high regardless of season

## 3. Enable Easy Rescheduling

Make it frictionless for patients to move their appointment rather than simply not showing up. A one-tap reschedule link in your reminder message converts a potential no-show into a kept appointment. Patients are more likely to reschedule than cancel entirely when the process is simple.

## 4. Offer Telehealth for Follow-Ups

Routine follow-ups and medication reviews do not always require an in-person visit. During Ramadan, offering a telehealth option for these appointments removes the barrier of travel and waiting rooms for fasting patients. Many patients who would skip an in-person visit will happily join a 10-minute video call from home.

## 5. Create a Ramadan-Specific Waitlist

When cancellations do happen, fill the slot immediately. A Ramadan waitlist of patients who prefer short-notice appointments lets you recover revenue from every cancellation. Many patients actually prefer this flexibility during the holy month.

## Measuring the Impact

Track these metrics during your Ramadan campaign:
- No-show rate by time slot (morning vs. afternoon vs. evening)
- Reschedule rate (what percentage of potential no-shows convert to rescheduled visits)
- Telehealth adoption rate for follow-ups
- Waitlist fill rate for cancelled slots

## Start Before Ramadan Begins

The most effective practices communicate these changes to patients before the first day of fasting. A simple message explaining your adjusted hours, telehealth options, and flexible scheduling sets expectations and builds goodwill.
