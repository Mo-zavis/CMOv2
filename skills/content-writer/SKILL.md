## Zavis Content Writing Rules (Non-Negotiable)

These rules override any default writing behavior. Every piece of content must comply.

### Absolute Rules
1. **No em dashes anywhere.** Never use the character. Use periods, commas, semicolons, or restructure the sentence instead.
2. **No emojis anywhere.** Never use emoji characters in any copy, UI text, labels, or content.
3. **No negative framing in headlines.** Never lead with "Stop losing...", "Don't let...", "Are you missing...". Headlines are affirmative statements.
4. **No tool-replacement positioning.** Never say "replaces 5-8 tools" or "consolidates your stack." Zavis IS the patient engagement infrastructure. It is not defined by what it replaces.
5. **No question-phrased headlines.** Headlines are statements, not questions. They declare, they do not ask.
6. **Concise feature descriptions.** 15 to 25 words. Never 40 to 55 words. Every word must earn its place.
7. **Self-QA before presenting.** Evaluate every output internally before showing it. Never present subpar work.

### The Identity Rule
Zavis is the AI-powered patient engagement platform for healthcare. Every piece of copy must reinforce this identity. When a reader finishes any page, they should understand: this is the platform that handles end-to-end patient engagement for healthcare organizations, powered by AI.

### The Mechanism Rule
Every claim must show HOW it works. Not just "increase revenue" but "AI agents respond to every inquiry within seconds, qualify patient intent, and book appointments directly into your EMR, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week." The mechanism is what makes the claim believable.

### The First-Principles Rule
Every sentence must pass the "So what?" test. If a sentence describes a feature without connecting it to increased revenue, reduced no-shows, or patient satisfaction, rewrite it or cut it.

**The formula:**
**What the platform does -> How it works (the mechanism) -> Which pillar it elevates (revenue / no-shows / satisfaction)**

### Writing Rule Details

1. **Headlines are affirmative statements.** They declare what Zavis is, what it does, or what outcome it delivers. Example: "Omnichannel Patient Communication Across WhatsApp, Instagram, Phone, and Web" not "Stop losing patients between channels."

2. **Every feature gets a pillar connection.** When describing any feature, explicitly tie it to increased revenue, reduced no-shows, or patient satisfaction. Use natural language. Make the connection unmistakable.

3. **Use "patient" not "customer"** (except veterinary: "pet parent/pet owner").

4. **Show the mechanism, then the outcome.** "AI agents respond to every inquiry within seconds, qualify intent, and book directly into your EMR. Leads that arrive at 2 AM are booked by 2:01 AM."

5. **Name the channels.** Say "WhatsApp, Instagram, and web chat" not "messaging channels." Specificity builds credibility and SEO value.

6. **Quantify with specifics.** "AI handles 60 to 90 percent of routine patient queries" not "AI handles most queries." Use real numbers wherever they exist.

7. **CTAs are action-oriented.** "Book a Demo" / "See Zavis in Action" / "Get Started." Clean, direct, no gimmicks.

8. **Ground everything in healthcare.** No generic SaaS language. Every example, every workflow, every metric should feel like it came from a real clinic or hospital.

9. **When describing AI:** emphasize what it accomplishes (instant response, 24/7 availability, intelligent booking, multilingual communication) and how it escalates to humans (under 10 seconds, full context preserved).

10. **When describing integrations:** emphasize the unified data layer (real-time sync, full attribution, one source of truth) not "eliminating tools."

11. **Stack the pillars when possible.** The most powerful copy hits two or three pillars at once. Example: "Automated appointment reminders via WhatsApp reduce no-shows, recover revenue from empty slots, and give patients a seamless rescheduling experience."

## Copy Hierarchy

Every page or content section follows this hierarchy:

1. **Headline:** Affirmative statement. What Zavis is or what it achieves. SEO keyword included naturally.
2. **Subhead:** How the mechanism works. One to two lines maximum.
3. **Body:** The mechanism in detail. What the platform does, how it does it, which pillar(s) it serves.
4. **CTA:** Action-oriented. "Book a Demo" or "See Zavis in Action."

## SEO Optimization Rules

### On-Page SEO
- Every page title and H1 must contain a primary keyword that healthcare organizations actively search for
- Use exact-match keywords naturally in H1, H2, and the first paragraph of every page
- Every page should be semantically complete: if someone reads only that page, they understand what Zavis is and what it does
- Include internal links to related Zavis pages (product features, use cases, resources)
- Meta descriptions: 150-160 characters, include primary keyword, include a CTA or value proposition
- URL slugs: lowercase, hyphenated, keyword-rich, no stop words

### Target Keyword Categories
- "patient engagement platform"
- "healthcare CRM"
- "AI healthcare" / "AI for healthcare"
- "reduce no-shows" / "reduce no-shows healthcare"
- "patient communication platform"
- "healthcare automation"
- "WhatsApp for healthcare"
- "EMR integration"
- "clinic management software"
- "patient booking system"
- "healthcare AI agents"
- "patient retention healthcare"
- "appointment reminder system"
- "healthcare omnichannel"

### Content-Type Specific SEO

**Blog Posts:**
- 1500-2500 words for pillar content, 800-1200 for supporting content
- One primary keyword per post, 3-5 secondary keywords
- Include H2s and H3s with keyword variations
- Add a table of contents for posts over 1500 words
- Include at least one internal link per 300 words

**Landing Pages:**
- H1 contains primary keyword exactly
- Above-the-fold copy addresses the core search intent in under 50 words
- Feature sections each target a secondary keyword
- Include FAQ section with question-based keywords

**Ad Copy:**
- Google Ads: 30-character headlines (keyword in headline 1), 90-character descriptions
- Meta Ads: 125-character primary text (above fold), 250-character body, 25-character headline

## AIO (AI Overview Optimization) Rules

Follow these rules to ensure content is cited by AI chatbots and appears in Google AI Overviews:

1. **Begin sections with clear entity definitions.** "Zavis is [definition]" so AI crawlers can extract and serve structured answers.
2. **Write in a factual, authoritative register** that AI systems prefer to cite. Avoid hedging language.
3. **Include structured claims with specific mechanisms** (not vague promises). "Zavis AI agents book appointments directly into your EMR within minutes of first contact" is citable. "Zavis helps with bookings" is not.
4. **Use question-answer patterns** in content where natural, since AI assistants surface content that directly answers user queries.
5. **Ensure rich semantic context:** mention related entities (EMR, WhatsApp, healthcare CRM, patient engagement) so AI systems understand the category and relationships.
6. **Every page should be independently crawlable** and make sense as a standalone answer to "What is Zavis?" or "How does [feature] work for healthcare?"

## Workflow Process

### Step 1: Keyword Research
Before writing anything:
- Pull keyword data from Ahrefs and SEMrush for the target topic
- Identify: primary keyword, secondary keywords, search intent (informational, commercial, transactional)
- Check keyword difficulty, monthly search volume, and SERP features
- Analyze competitor content ranking for target keywords
- Identify content gaps (topics competitors rank for that Zavis does not)

### Step 2: Brief Creation
Create a structured content brief:
```yaml
brief:
  type: "blog_post"  # blog_post | landing_page | ad_copy | email_copy | social_caption | press_release
  primary_keyword: "reduce no-shows healthcare"
  secondary_keywords: ["appointment reminder system", "patient no-show solutions", "WhatsApp appointment reminders"]
  search_intent: "commercial"
  word_count_target: 2000
  outline:
    - h2: "Why Healthcare No-Shows Cost More Than Empty Chairs"
    - h2: "How Automated Reminders via WhatsApp Reduce No-Shows"
    - h2: "The Zavis Approach: AI-Powered Booking Lifecycle Management"
    - h2: "Results: What Clinics See After Implementing Zavis"
    - h2: "Getting Started with Automated Appointment Reminders"
  cta: "Book a demo to see how Zavis reduces no-shows at your clinic"
  internal_links: ["/features/automations", "/features/ai-agents", "/features/bookings"]
  pillar_connection: "reduce_no_shows"
```

### Step 3: Write
Write the content following:
- Brand voice and tone from zavis-master
- All absolute writing rules (no em dashes, no emojis, no negative headlines, etc.)
- Copy hierarchy (headline, subhead, body, CTA)
- SEO guidelines (keyword placement, heading structure, internal links)
- AIO optimization rules (entity definitions, structured claims, Q&A patterns)
- Pillar-specific language bank for relevant content
- Store draft as markdown in `/assets/copy/{asset-id}/v1.md`

### Step 4: SEO Scoring
Run the draft through Surfer SEO (or equivalent):
- Check keyword density and placement
- Verify heading structure (H1, H2, H3 hierarchy)
- Assess readability score
- Check content length against target
- Identify missing keywords or topics
- Optimize until score meets threshold (target: 80+ Surfer score)

### Step 5: Store with Metadata
```yaml
asset:
  id: "copy-2026-03-04-001"
  campaign_id: "campaign-q2-2026-pe-launch"
  type: "blog_post"
  version: 1
  status: IN_REVIEW
  created: "2026-03-04"

seo:
  primary_keyword: "reduce no-shows healthcare"
  secondary_keywords: ["appointment reminder system", "patient no-show solutions"]
  surfer_score: 84
  word_count: 2150
  readability: "grade_8"

brand_compliance:
  em_dashes: false
  emojis: false
  negative_headlines: false
  pillar_connection: "reduce_no_shows"
  patient_not_customer: true
  mechanism_shown: true

files:
  draft: "v1.md"
  metadata: "metadata.yaml"
```

### Step 6: Human Review
Present content for feedback:
- Show the full content with section highlights
- Display SEO score and keyword analysis
- Side-by-side diff for revisions (v1 vs. v2)
- Flag any sections where reviewers may want to provide specific feedback
- Include brand compliance checklist results

### Step 7: Iterate
Process feedback and revise:
- Address all reviewer comments
- Re-run SEO scoring after changes
- Save as new version (v2.md, v3.md)
- Update metadata with version history

### Step 8: Publish to CMS
Once approved:
- Push to WordPress, Ghost, or Webflow via their APIs
- Set: meta title, meta description, featured image, categories, tags, slug
- Verify: Open Graph tags, Twitter Card tags, canonical URL
- Set publish date (immediate or scheduled)
- Mark as `PUBLISHED` in campaign status

### Step 9: Monitor Performance
After publishing:
- Track via Google Search Console: impressions, clicks, CTR, average position
- Monitor keyword rankings via Ahrefs/SEMrush
- Feed performance data to analytics-reporter and seo-optimizer skills
- Schedule content refresh if rankings plateau or decline

## Content Type Templates

### Blog Post Structure
```
# [H1: Primary Keyword in Affirmative Statement]

[Opening paragraph: 50-80 words, includes primary keyword, establishes the topic and why it matters to healthcare organizations]

## [H2: Problem Context / Why This Matters]
[200-300 words establishing the healthcare-specific problem]

## [H2: The Mechanism / How It Works]
[300-400 words showing how Zavis solves this, with specific mechanisms]

## [H2: Feature Deep Dive]
[300-400 words on specific Zavis features with pillar connections]

## [H2: Results / What to Expect]
[200-300 words with metrics, outcomes, and proof points]

## [H2: Getting Started]
[100-150 words with CTA: Book a Demo]

---
[Internal links to related content]
```

### Ad Copy Structure (Google Ads)
```
Headline 1 (30 chars): [Primary keyword + brand]
Headline 2 (30 chars): [Key benefit / mechanism]
Headline 3 (30 chars): [CTA or proof point]
Description 1 (90 chars): [Mechanism + outcome + pillar connection]
Description 2 (90 chars): [Secondary benefit + CTA]
```

### Social Caption Structure
```
[Opening line: bold statement or insight, no questions]
[1-2 sentences: mechanism or specific detail]
[CTA: link, action, or engagement prompt]
[Hashtags (platform-appropriate)]
```
