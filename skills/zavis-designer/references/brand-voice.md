# Zavis Brand Voice — Tone, Messaging & Content Rules

> How Zavis speaks across every medium. Apply these rules to all copy,
> headlines, descriptions, CTAs, social posts, ad copy, and video scripts.

---

## 1. Brand Personality

**Core Traits**:
- Professional, warm, and approachable
- Healthcare-first (never generic SaaS)
- Confident without being aggressive
- Specific over vague — use real metrics, real workflows, real outcomes
- Trust-inspiring — "a premium tool you'd trust with your patients"

**Think**: A brilliant operations lead at a top healthcare group who genuinely
cares about patient outcomes AND knows their tech inside-out.

**Never**: Cold corporate, generic startup, aggressive sales, clinical/sterile.

## 2. Language Rules

### Terminology

| Use This              | Not This                    |
|-----------------------|-----------------------------|
| Patient               | Customer (except veterinary)|
| Pet parent / pet owner| Customer (veterinary)       |
| Practice / clinic     | Business / company          |
| Healthcare team       | Staff / employees           |
| Patient success       | Customer success            |
| Go live               | Deploy / launch             |
| Book a Demo           | Sign Up / Get Started       |
| See It in Action      | Learn More (generic)        |

### Feature Description Formula

Every feature description follows this pattern:
1. **What it does** — one clear sentence
2. **How it works** — mechanics, channels, integrations
3. **What outcome it drives** — metric, time saved, conversion improved

Example:
> "AI Agents handle 60-90% of patient inquiries across WhatsApp, Instagram,
> and web chat. They understand intent, search live EMR schedules, and book
> appointments with two-way writeback — escalating to humans in under 10
> seconds when needed."

### Numbers & Metrics

Always be specific. Strong numbers build trust:
- "60-90%" (not "most")
- "<10 seconds" (not "quickly")
- "24 hours" (not "fast")
- "2-way writeback" (not "integration")
- Name channels: "WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook" (not "messaging apps")

### CTA Hierarchy

| Level    | Text                    | Usage                      |
|----------|-------------------------|----------------------------|
| Primary  | Book a Demo             | Hero sections, end-of-page |
| Primary  | See It in Action        | Feature highlights         |
| Secondary| Learn More              | Feature cards (links)      |
| Tertiary | Contact Us              | Footer, about page         |

### Headline Patterns

- **Problem → Solution**: "Stop losing patients to phone tag. Start converting with AI."
- **Metric-Led**: "60-90% of patient ops, automated."
- **Benefit-First**: "One inbox for every patient conversation."
- **Question**: "Ready to transform your practice?"
- **Green Accent**: Always highlight 1-2 key words in `<span class="text-[#006828]">green</span>`

### Description Patterns

- Max 2 sentences for hero descriptions
- Max 1 sentence for card descriptions
- Always Geist font, text-sm or text-[13px]
- Always muted opacity (black/50 or black/45)
- End with outcome, not feature
- **Max 15 to 25 words for feature card descriptions.** Cut ruthlessly. Every word must earn its place.

## 4. Specialty-Specific Messaging

### Common Pattern

Each specialty page follows: `[Specialty] practices trust Zavis to [primary benefit]`

### Dental (Primary)
- Focus: Treatment acceptance, patient retention, operatory efficiency
- Tone: Confident, modern — "dentistry doesn't have to feel dated"
- Metrics: Treatment acceptance rates, chair time, re-booking rates

### Dermatology
- Focus: Consultation conversion, treatment plan follow-through
- Tone: Premium, aesthetic — matches the specialty's patient expectations

### Optometry
- Focus: Appointment booking, frame selection follow-up, recall cycles
- Tone: Clear, precise — "see the difference" messaging works well

### Orthopedics
- Focus: Surgical consultation conversion, post-op follow-up, rehab adherence
- Tone: Strong, reliable — emphasize recovery journey support

### ENT
- Focus: Referral management, multi-condition handling, follow-up care
- Tone: Thorough, attentive — multiple conditions per patient

### Urgent Care
- Focus: Wait time communication, triage, rapid patient flow
- Tone: Efficient, responsive — speed is the differentiator

### Mental Health
- Focus: Sensitive patient communication, appointment adherence, crisis protocols
- Tone: Empathetic, careful — privacy and sensitivity paramount

### Veterinary
- Focus: Pet parent communication, multi-pet management, preventive care reminders
- Tone: Caring, family-oriented — "pet parent" not "customer"

#### Homecare
- **Focus:** Coordinating in-home visits, caregiver scheduling, remote patient monitoring
- **Tone:** Compassionate, family-oriented, emphasizing dignity and comfort
- **Key metrics:** Visit coordination efficiency, caregiver utilization, patient family satisfaction

#### Aesthetic
- **Focus:** Treatment consultations, appointment scheduling, follow-up care for cosmetic procedures
- **Tone:** Premium, confidence-building, results-oriented
- **Key metrics:** Consultation-to-booking conversion, treatment plan adherence, patient retention

#### Longevity & Wellness
- **Focus:** Preventive care programs, wellness check-ins, ongoing health monitoring
- **Tone:** Forward-thinking, proactive, holistic health partner
- **Key metrics:** Program enrollment rates, patient engagement frequency, wellness outcome tracking

## 5. Content for Different Mediums

### Website Copy
- Short, punchy headlines (Bricolage Grotesque)
- 1-2 sentence descriptions max
- Green accent on 1-2 key words per heading
- Feature lists use check icons, not bullets
- Always end sections with clear next step

### Social Media Copy
- **LinkedIn**: Professional, data-driven, industry insight angle
  - Lead with a metric or insight
  - 3-4 short paragraphs
  - End with question or CTA
- **Instagram**: Visual-first, benefit-focused, shorter copy
  - 1-2 sentences max in image
  - Caption expands the story
  - Use relevant healthcare hashtags
- **Twitter/X**: Punchy, one-liner with stat or provocative question
  - Under 200 characters ideal
  - Link to longer content

### Ad Copy
- **Google Ads**: Keyword-dense, benefit-first, specific metrics
  - Headline: "AI Patient Communication for [Specialty]"
  - Description: "60-90% automation. Go live in 24 hours. Book a Demo."
- **Meta Ads**: Pain point → solution, emotional + rational
  - "Tired of missed calls becoming missed patients?"
  - "Zavis AI responds in seconds, books in real-time."
- **LinkedIn Ads**: Professional, ROI-focused, authority-building
  - "Healthcare organizations using Zavis see 60-90% automation of routine ops."

### Email Copy
- Subject: Direct benefit or specific metric (no clickbait)
- Body: Problem → solution → proof → CTA
- Max 3 short paragraphs
- One clear CTA button
- Geist font throughout

### Video Scripts
- Open with the pain point (5 seconds)
- Show the solution (10-15 seconds)
- Prove it with metrics (5 seconds)
- CTA (5 seconds)
- Total: 25-30 seconds ideal for ads, 60-90 for product demos
